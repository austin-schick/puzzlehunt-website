CMU 15-112 Spring 2018: Fundamentals of Programming and Computer Science
Lab 3 (Friday 2-Feb)

These problems should be completed during your scheduled lab on Friday. To get full credit on the lab portion of the homework, you must complete the basic problem, attempt the advanced problem, and show your work to one of the lab TAs. You may work on these problems collaboratively or individually, as you wish (unless the instructions state otherwise).

Do not use lists or recursion this week. Do not hardcode the test cases in your solutions.

  1. Basic 1: Pair Programming!
    Pair programming is a process where two people code together simultaneously. One person is the driver: they sit at the computer and actually type the code. The other is the navigator: they sit next to the driver and direct the coding process by suggesting what should be done next and reviewing each line of code. The pair switches roles every so often to make sure that they both get a chance to try both roles; in 15-112, we'll start by switching roles every five minutes (as announced by the TAs).

    Find a partner and use pair programming to write the function nthFunkyNumber(n). We define a funky number to be a positive number where the ones digit is divisible by 1, the tens digit is divisible by 2, the hundreds digit is divisible by 3, and so on. This means that 1-9 are all funky, as are 20-29, 300-309, etc. We assume that any provided number will have 9 or fewer digits. For this function, you may not cast numbers to strings.

    You have up to 20 minutes to work on this problem. After that point, the TAs will check your work, give credit to everyone who has used effective collaboration, and will move you on to the next problem.
